# Product

## Register

product

## Users

UK club rowers and club captains / coaches, equally. Athletes check their own and rivals' GMT% after regattas; coaches use club and regatta-level data to understand performance trends over a season. Both are experienced with rowing performance metrics and expect data presented at expert level, not simplified for a general audience.

There is also a public-facing discovery surface: visitors who find the site via search and need to understand what GMT% is and why it matters before they engage with the tool.

## Product Purpose

RowingTools calculates GMT% (a world-benchmark percentage for rowing race times) and aggregates UK club and regatta performance data. It lets rowers and coaches contextualise any result against a consistent benchmark, browse club rankings, and track performance across seasons.

Success: a rower can go from a raw race time to a meaningful GMT% and a club comparison in under a minute.

## Brand Personality

Understated, expert, craft. Built by a rower, for rowers. The product earns credibility through precision, not promotion. It never oversells itself.

## Anti-references

- British Rowing official site: institutional, stiff, government-y. This should feel independent and personal, not bureaucratic.
- Generic sports analytics SaaS: gradient-heavy dashboards, hero metrics, stock photography. RowingTools has no marketing budget and doesn't pretend otherwise.

## Design Principles

1. **The data is the interface.** Every visual decision should make numbers easier to read, not compete with them. Decoration earns its place or gets cut.
2. **Earned accent.** The brand red appears only where it signals something meaningful: the brand mark, a focus state, a performance category. Restraint makes it land.
3. **Expert defaults.** Present data in the form an experienced rower expects. No tooltips explaining what GMT% means on every screen; trust the audience.
4. **Show, don't explain.** The interface makes GMT% legible through layout and hierarchy, not tutorial copy.
5. **Quiet confidence.** The design can be distinctive without being loud. Fraunces at 900 weight does the heavy lifting; the rest stays controlled.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard good practice. Dark mode is already supported via `prefers-color-scheme`. Maintain adequate contrast ratios for data-dense tables. No formal WCAG compliance target stated.
