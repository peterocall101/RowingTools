Caveats - how to read these rankings
  1. World Best Times are 2000m flat-water benchmarks, so given Henley's stream conditions and different distance markers, they're included only to allow comparison between crews, not as an absolute statement of speed.
  2. Leading crews at Henley typically ease off before the line when the gap to the losing crew is large, so comparing crews at the Barrier and Fawley can be more insightful - especially on earlier days, where there can be larger margins of victory.
  3. Conditions at Henley can vary significantly within a single day - that's why the weather conditions can be viewed for each race.
  4. Henley record benchmarks are the records to each distance marker going into the regatta, so it's possible to score over 100%. The benchmarks do not change over the course of the regatta.
