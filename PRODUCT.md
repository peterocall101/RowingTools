# Product

## Register

brand

(The homepage and section landings are the brand surface; the calculator, leaderboards and club pages are product surfaces that share the same shell.)

## Users

UK club rowers, coaches and club captains. They arrive from a WhatsApp link or a Google search after a regatta, often on a phone at the boathouse or on the towpath, wanting to know how fast their crew really was and how their club compares.

## Product Purpose

RowingTools turns raw regatta times into one comparable number: GMT% (benchmark time / your time x 100, scored against World Best Times and UK benchmarks). It covers a GMT% calculator, per-regatta heatmaps and leaderboards, club profiles aggregated across regattas, and a live Henley Royal Regatta ranking. Success = a rower trusts the number enough to quote it to their crew.

## Brand Personality

Timed, ruled, matter-of-fact. The visual reference is the physical object of the sport: the printed regatta programme and the finish-line results board. Confident large type, hairline rules, tabular numerals, one decisive red. Copy states facts (counts, dates, distances) instead of adjectives.

## Anti-references

- The soft SaaS card grid: rounded cards, emoji icons, drop shadows, muted gray-on-gray (this was the previous design, described by the owner as "wishy washy").
- Generic sports-app gradients and glassmorphism.
- Corporate rowing-federation sites: dense nav bars, carousels, stock photos of sunsets.

Positive references named by the owner: Pentagram (typographic confidence, ruled index), Hiut Denim (honest, specific copy), The Juggernaut (editorial sharpness, one red), Twelve Labs (technical precision).

## Design Principles

1. **Results-board, not brochure.** Every surface should look like it could be pinned up at a finish line: rules, columns, tabular figures.
2. **The number is the hero.** GMT% is the product; typography should let a percentage or a time carry a whole section.
3. **Specific beats superlative.** "13 regattas, 4,009 results, 450+ clubs" beats any adjective.
4. **Sharp by default.** Square corners, hairline borders, no decorative shadows. Contrast does the emphasis.
5. **Dark stays dark.** The data pages are dark; the shell stays dark so navigation never flashes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target, but body text >= 4.5:1 on its background, large display text >= 3:1, reduced-motion alternatives for all entrance animation. Used heavily on phones outdoors, so favour high contrast.
